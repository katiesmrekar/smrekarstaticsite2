Digital literacy narrative 1

The first project of the semester was to create a website using any technology or program that describes our digital literacy narrative throughout the years. I chose to craft my website using Google sites due to my familiarity of the program and its usability. This program can be easily learned and used, but offers template-based webpages which decrease the individualistic quality of websites. I created my Google site and it looked as follows:

I thought this project was a great project for the course to begin with. Students were able to deeply investigate their digital literacy and what technologies got them to where they are now. This project helped me to realize that I am proficient in many technologies due to the time period I was born in. I had a computer growing up, I got a smartphone in middle school, and every grade school I attended had Macbooks. I was fortunate enough to learn various technologies from a young age which made my digital literacy strong before this class had started. Static site 1

In the beginning of the Fall 2020 semester, the students of Writing with digital technologies were assigned to create a website using HTML. I had little experience with HTML, so I was excited to learn more about the program and experiment with its functionalities. Eventually, I created my website and it looked as follows:

The creation of this website taught me the basics of HTML. I also learned more about rhetorical devices in website creation. HTML offers users to create websites from scratch while other platforms such as Wix or Google sites have templates to choose from. Using a mass market content management system takes away a website's opportunity for individualism and also creates less room for rhetorical devices. I believe that while HTML may be more strenuous, it offers web designers more flexibility and creativity than that of a template-based system. At the end of the semester, the class was assigned to create another static site to observe our progress in using digital technologies. The current webpage you are on acts as my end of the year, static site 2.
